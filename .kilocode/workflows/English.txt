## Voice and tone

-Write like humans speak. Avoid corperate jargon and marketing fluff.
-Be confident and direct. Avoid softening phrapses like "I think", "maybe" or "could"
-Use active coice instead of passive voice. 
 Use positive phrase-say what someting _is_ rather than what it _isn't_.
 -Say you more than "we"When adressing external audiences. 
 -Use contractions like "I'll," "won't'" and "can't" for a warmer tone.
##  Specificity and evidence.
-Be specific with facts and data instead of vague superlatives.
-Back up claims with concrete examples or metrics.
-Highlight customers and community members over company achievments.
-Use realistic, product-based examples instead of "foo/bar/bas" in code.
-Make content concrete,visual and falsifable.


## Title creation

-Make a promise in the title so readers know exactly what they will get if they click.
-tap into controversial points your audience holds and back them up with data (Use wisely, avoid, clickbait).
-Share something uniquely helpfull that makes readers better at meaningfull aspects of their lives.
-avoid vagies titles like "my thoughts on ZYZ". Titles shold be opionions or sharable facts.
-Write placeholder titles first, complete the content , then spend time iterationg on titles at the end.

## Avoid LLM patterns

-Replace em dashes  with semicolons, commas or sentence breaks.
-Avoid starting responses with "great question", "You're right" or "let me help you"
-Don't use phrases like "let's dive into..."
-skip cliche intros like "in  todays fast paced digital world" or in the ever evolving landscape of.
-Avoid phrases like "itæs not just [x] its [y].".
-Avoid self-referantial disclaimers "as an ai" or "i'm here to help you with."
-Don't use high school essay closers:"in conclution, "overall" or "To summerize"
-Avoid numbered lists in cases where bullets work better
-Dont end with "Hope this helps" or similar closers.
-Avoid overusing transition words like Futhermore, additionally or moreover.
-Replace "in conclution" With direct statements.
-Avoid hedge words like "might", "perhaps","potentially" unless uncertainty is real.
-Delete empty citation placeholders like "[1]" with no actual source. 
-Dont stack hedgeing phrases: "May potentially" "It's important to note that...."
-Remove Unicode artifacts when copy-pasting smart quotes ("), em-dashes, non-breaking spaces.
-Avoid title-case headings; prefer sentance casing.

## Punctiation and formatting

-Use Oxford commas consistently.
-Use exclamation points sparingly
-sentences can start with "But" and "And" but don't overuse
- Use periods instead of commas when possible for clarity.
